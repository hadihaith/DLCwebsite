About
The DLC

The Dean's list council was first established on March 30, 1998, on behalf of the dean of College of Business Administration. It is clear from the name of this council that the members are on the dean's list, which is the list that is established to recognize outstanding honor students. However, not only must members of DLC show academic excellence but are required to demonstrate excellence in the area of leadership, scholarship, service, and character. These characteristics have been associated with membership in this council. Since DLC is more than just academic recognition, members are highly spirited, creative, social, and are determined to make the College of Business Administration and its community a better place to be in. Teamwork is an evident character within members of DLC where they work earnestly with faculty, students and different organizations inside and outside Kuwait University. Simply DLC shares and cares. The members of the List Council are high-profile students, distinct and successful in their studies and thus meriting recognition by the Dean. The main goals of the Council are to create a sense of effective competition among students, encourage honor students to excel in CBA, build a stronger relationship between faculty, administration and students and -above all -provide suggestions, opinions and complaints of honor students about academic issues and faculty, important feedback in continuous improvement at CBA.

Our goals?

  • To create a sense of effective competition among students and encourage honor students.
  • To build a stronger relationship between faculty and honor students.
  • To build a stronger relationship between honor student and the rest of the student body.
  • To build a stronger relationship between honor students of College of Business Administration and other honor students of other colleges.
  • To let honor students, represent the College of Business Administration in most student and societal ceremonies. This can be both inside and outside Kuwait.
  • To take into consideration the suggestions, opinions and complaints of honor students about academic issues, school spirit and faculty.
  • To organize and come up with activities that they think will be effective in changing the college's spirit and are beneficial.
